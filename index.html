DroneVLA: Fine-Tuned π0 for Generalist Aerial
Manipulation and Navigation

Anonymous Authors
Paper arXiv Code Data

DroneVLA studies how a manipulation-pretrained VLA transfers to aerial navigation and pick-and-place. We combine fine-tuning with inference-time Physics-Aware Guidance and Real-Time Chunking to address flight dynamics, and use Gaussian Splatting to synthesize training data.

Abstract

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) foundation models such as π0 show strong cross-embodiment generalization for fixed-base manipulation, but transferring them to aerial platforms is challenging due to underactuated, highly dynamic flight. We introduce DroneVLA, a system study of fine-tuning manipulation-pretrained VLAs for aerial navigation and pick-and-place. We find that while visual representations transfer effectively, flight control dynamics do not. To bridge this “dynamics gap” without retraining the foundation model, we add Physics-Aware Guidance during flow-matching sampling to inject tracking and payload constraints. We also use a Gaussian Splatting pipeline to synthesize training data. In real-world experiments, Physics-Aware Guidance + Real-Time Chunking improve navigation success from 30% → 65%, and in synthetic settings reach 95% success where direct fine-tuning fails. However, long-horizon compositional tasks still fail completely (0%), suggesting current VLA architectures struggle with high-level sequencing after domain-specific fine-tuning.

Method Overview

  • Fine-tuning a flow-matching VLA policy on aerial navigation and pick-and-place.
  • Real-Time Chunking (RTC) to synchronize inference with execution and reduce action discontinuities.
  • Physics-Aware Guidance to steer sampling with tracking-error objectives and payload-aware vertical support.
  • Gaussian Splatting to synthesize training environments and demonstrations for broader coverage.

Results Highlights

  • Real-world navigation: Physics-Aware Guidance + RTC improves success from 30% → 65%.
  • Synthetic navigation: Guidance + RTC reaches 95% success where direct fine-tuning fails.
  • Compositional tasks: Long-horizon navigate→pick→place remains at 0% success after fine-tuning.
